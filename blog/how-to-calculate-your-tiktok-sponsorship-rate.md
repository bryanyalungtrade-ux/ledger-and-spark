## Why Sponsorship Rates Matter

Your rate isn't just a number. It sets the tone for every brand relationship you'll ever have.

If you undercharge:
- Brands see you as "cheap" and treat your content accordingly
- You burn out creating sponsored content that doesn't pay the bills
- You attract low-quality brands with tiny budgets

If you overcharge:
- Brands ghost you
- You lose deals to competitors who priced right

The sweet spot? Knowing the actual market rate for your size, platform, and niche.

## The Base Rate Formula

Brands start with a simple baseline:

**Base rate = [Followers / 1,000] x [Platform CPM]**

CPM (Cost Per Mille) varies by platform:

| Platform | CPM Range | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| TikTok | $12-$20 per 1K followers | Highest organic reach, youngest audience |
| Instagram Reel | $10-$15 per 1K followers | Strong for brand aesthetic, Stories add value |
| Instagram Post | $10 per 1K followers | Static, lower reach than Reels |
| YouTube Video | $20-$50 per 1K subscribers | Evergreen content, highest lifetime value |
| YouTube Shorts | $5-$15 per 1K subscribers | Short lifespan but growing |
| Twitter/X Post | $2-$5 per 1K followers | Shortest content lifespan |

**Example:** 25,000 TikTok followers at $15 CPM = $375 base rate per video.

But the base rate is just the starting point.

## How Your Numbers Multiply Your Rate

This is where most creators leave money on the table.

Your follower count is the FLOOR. How your posts are doing is the CEILING.

A creator with 25K followers and 7% interaction rate is worth way more than someone with 25K followers and 1%. Brands know this.

**How to figure out your multiplier:**

| How Your Posts Are Doing | Multiplier |
|----------------|------------|
| Below 2% | 0.8x (below average) |
| 2-4% | 1.0x (average) |
| 4-6% | 1.2x (above average) |
| 6-8% | 1.4x (strong) |
| 8%+ | 1.6x+ (exceptional) |

**Updated example:** 25K followers, $15 CPM, 5% interaction rate (1.2x multiplier)
$375 x 1.2 = **$450 per TikTok video**

Not all niches are created equal. Brands in some categories have bigger budgets.

- Technology & SaaS
- Beauty & skincare
- Fitness & wellness

**Average-paying niches (base rate):**

**Lower-paying niches (may need to discount 10-15%):**
- Comedy & entertainment
- General lifestyle
- Memes & viral content

**Final example:** 25K TikTok followers, 5% interaction rate, beauty niche
$375 (base) x 1.2 (interaction) x 1.15 (niche) = **$517 per TikTok video**

Your realistic range: $450-$600 per video.

## How to Actually Negotiate

Knowing your rate is step one. Using it is step two.

### Rule 1: Let Them Anchor First
When a brand asks "What's your rate?" — try to deflect first: "I'd love to learn more about the campaign. What's the budget you're working with?"

If they give you a number first, you know their ceiling.

### Rule 2: If You Quote First, Quote Your Mid-Range
Don't start with your lowest number. Quote the middle of your range and leave room to negotiate.

### Rule 3: Offer Packages
One TikTok: $500
Three TikToks over 30 days: $1,300 (small discount, way more total revenue)

Brands love packages. You make more money. Win-win.

### Rule 4: Know Your Non-Negotiables
- Usage rights (can they reuse your content in ads? That costs extra, usually 2-3x)
- Exclusivity (can't promote competitors? That costs extra)
- Timeline (rush jobs cost more)
- Revisions (set a limit, usually 1-2 rounds)

### Rule 5: Get Everything in Writing
Deliverables, timeline, payment terms, usage rights. No handshake deals. This is business.

## Red Flags From Brands

Watch out for these:
- "We'll pay you in exposure" — Run.
- "We have a $50 budget for a full video" — Counter with your real rate or walk.
- "We need the video tomorrow" — Rush fee.
- "We want full usage rights forever" — That's a separate (large) fee.
- They won't sign a contract — Not a serious brand.
