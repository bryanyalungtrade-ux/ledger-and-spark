How to Plan a Week of Content in 10 Minutes (Free Tool Inside)

Ledger & Spark • March 2026 • 6 min read

You know that feeling when you sit down to post and... nothing? Your brain is empty. You scroll for 20 minutes looking for inspiration. Then you just don't post.

Sound familiar?

You're not lazy. You're not out of ideas. You just don't have a plan. And without a plan, every single day feels like starting from zero.

But here's the good news: planning your content doesn't have to take hours. You don't need a fancy Notion template. You don't need a $40 spreadsheet from Etsy. You literally just need 10 minutes and a free tool.

Let's break it down.

Why Planning Actually Matters

Here's the harsh truth: if you don't post consistently, the algorithm forgets you exist. It's not personal. It's just how it works.

TikTok, Instagram, YouTube — they all reward creators who show up regularly. When you post every day or every other day, the algorithm starts pushing your stuff to more people. When you disappear for a week? Your reach tanks.

But consistency doesn't mean being chained to your phone 24/7. It means knowing what you're going to post before you need to post it. That's it. That's the whole secret.

Planning ahead = less stress, better content, and way more posts that actually go live instead of sitting in your drafts forever.

The 3-Step Method

Forget color-coded calendars and 47-tab spreadsheets. You only need to answer three questions:

  1. What's your niche? — Food, fitness, beauty, tech, lifestyle, comedy... whatever your thing is. Pick one. (You can have sub-niches, but start with one main topic.)
  2. Where do you post? — TikTok? Instagram? Both? YouTube Shorts? Pick your platforms. Don't try to be everywhere at once if you're just starting out.
  3. How often can you actually post? — Be real with yourself. 3 times a week is better than saying "every day" and burning out by Wednesday. Start with what you can stick to.

That's your entire content strategy. Niche + platforms + frequency. Everything else builds on top of that.

What a Week Actually Looks Like

Let's say you're a foodie creator posting 5 times a week on TikTok and Instagram. Here's what your generated week might look like:

  • Monday: "What I Ate Today" vlog — TikTok (film everything you eat, quick cuts, trending audio)
  • Tuesday: Restaurant review reel — Instagram Reel (30-second review of a local spot, rate it 1-10)
  • Wednesday: Recipe tutorial — TikTok (simple recipe, overhead shots, step-by-step)
  • Thursday: "Is it worth the hype?" — TikTok (try a trending food item, give your honest take)
  • Friday: Weekend food guide carousel — Instagram (top 5 places to eat this weekend in your city)

See how each day has a specific idea, format, and platform? No more "I don't know what to post." You just wake up, check your plan, and go.

Pro Tips to Make It Even Easier

Batch film on one day. Pick a day (Sunday works great) and film 3-5 videos in one sitting. Change your outfit between takes so it looks like different days. Edit them throughout the week and schedule them out.

Repurpose across platforms. That TikTok you filmed? Post it as an Instagram Reel too. Turn it into a YouTube Short. Screenshot the best frame and make it a carousel. One piece of content can live in 4 places with minimal extra work.

Don't overthink it. Your first take is usually your best take. The "perfect" video doesn't exist. The video you actually post will always beat the one you spent 3 hours perfecting and never uploaded.

Use your phone's calendar app. Once you generate your content plan, drop each idea into your phone calendar with a reminder. Free, simple, and you'll get a notification when it's time to post.

Stop Staring at a Blank Screen

Planning content doesn't have to be complicated. You don't need a course on it. You don't need to pay for templates. You just need to know your niche, pick your platforms, decide how often, and let the tool handle the rest.
