What Brands Actually Pay Creators in 2026 (Real Numbers)

Ledger & Spark • March 2026 • 9 min read

Everyone wants to know what other creators charge. It's the most searched, most whispered, most gatekept topic in the entire creator economy.

So we're just gonna say it. Out loud. With real numbers.

These rates come from industry reports, creator surveys, and what brands actually pay in 2026. We're not making this up, and we're not gatekeeping it behind a $49 course. Scroll down, find your follower count, and see what you should be charging.

How Brand Deals Actually Work

Before we get into the numbers, let's quickly cover how this works. Because a lot of creators get their first brand DM and completely freeze.

Here's the basic flow:

  1. A brand reaches out (or you pitch them)
  2. They tell you what they want (post, story, video, etc.)
  3. You tell them your rate
  4. You negotiate (yes, you can negotiate)
  5. You create the content, they pay you

The part where most creators mess up? Step 3. They either lowball themselves because they're scared to lose the deal, or they have literally no idea what the going rate is.

That's what this article fixes.

Creator Rates by Follower Count (2026)

These are average rates per single piece of sponsored content. Actual rates vary by niche, engagement, platform, and usage rights. But these numbers give you a solid baseline.

Nano Creators: 1K - 10K Followers

  • TikTok video: $50 - $250
  • Instagram Reel: $50 - $300
  • Instagram Story (3 slides): $25 - $100
  • YouTube Shorts: $50 - $200
  • YouTube video (dedicated): $200 - $500
  • Twitter/X post: $20 - $100

If you have under 10K followers, you can absolutely still get paid. Brands love nano creators because your engagement rates are usually way higher than big accounts. Your audience actually trusts you.

Micro Creators: 10K - 50K Followers

  • TikTok video: $200 - $800
  • Instagram Reel: $300 - $1,000
  • Instagram Story (3 slides): $100 - $400
  • YouTube Shorts: $200 - $600
  • YouTube video (dedicated): $500 - $2,500
  • Twitter/X post: $100 - $400

This is the sweet spot for most creators. You're big enough that brands take you seriously, but small enough that your rates are accessible to mid-size companies. If you're here and not getting brand deals, the issue is probably your pitch, not your size.

Mid-Tier Creators: 50K - 500K Followers

  • TikTok video: $800 - $5,000
  • Instagram Reel: $1,000 - $7,000
  • Instagram Story (3 slides): $400 - $1,500
  • YouTube Shorts: $500 - $3,000
  • YouTube video (dedicated): $2,500 - $15,000
  • Twitter/X post: $300 - $2,000

Macro Creators: 500K - 1M+ Followers

  • TikTok video: $5,000 - $25,000+
  • Instagram Reel: $7,000 - $30,000+
  • Instagram Story (3 slides): $1,500 - $5,000
  • YouTube Shorts: $3,000 - $10,000
  • YouTube video (dedicated): $15,000 - $50,000+
  • Twitter/X post: $2,000 - $10,000+

At this level, you're not just getting paid for a post. You're getting paid for reach, trust, and conversion. Many macro creators also negotiate usage rights, exclusivity periods, and multi-post packages that push total deal value way above these per-post numbers.

What Makes Your Rate Go Up (or Down)

Follower count is just the starting point. Here's what actually moves the needle on your rate:

Things That Increase Your Rate

  • High engagement rate. If your audience actually interacts with your posts (3%+ on IG, 5%+ on TikTok), you can charge more. Use our Clout Check tool to calculate yours.
  • Niche audience. Finance, tech, beauty, and health niches pay more because the products being advertised have higher margins.
  • Proven conversions. If you can show a brand that your last sponsored post drove actual sales, your rate skyrockets.
  • Professional media kit. A clean, well-designed media kit signals that you're serious. Brands pay more when you look like a professional. Build one free with our Media Kit Generator.
  • Exclusivity. If a brand wants you to not promote competitors for 30-90 days, that's worth extra. Usually 30-50% more.
  • Usage rights. Brand wants to use your content in their ads? That's a whole separate fee. Typically 50-100% on top of the base rate.

Things That Lower Your Rate

  • Low engagement. 500K followers but only 200 likes per post? Brands see right through that.
  • No media kit. If you can't clearly present your stats and value, brands assume you're amateur and offer bottom rates.
  • Desperation. If you immediately accept the first offer, you'll always get lowballed. Always counter. Always.
  • Fake followers. Brands use tools to check. If your followers are bought, you're done.

The Media Kit Trick That Gets You Higher Rates

Here's something most creators don't know: brands decide how much to offer you before they even message you. They check your profile, estimate your stats, and come in with a budget range already in mind.

A media kit flips this. When you reply to a brand inquiry with a professional media kit that shows your stats, audience demographics, past collabs, and your rates, you immediately set the anchor higher.

Psychology 101: whoever sets the first number wins the negotiation. Your media kit is that first number.

3 Negotiation Tips That Actually Work

  1. Never accept the first offer. The first number is always their floor. Counter with 20-30% higher and see what happens. Worst case, they meet you in the middle.
  2. Bundle deliverables. Instead of one TikTok for $500, offer a package: one TikTok + 3 Instagram Stories + one post for $1,200. Brands love packages because it feels like a deal. You make more per brand.
  3. Ask about usage rights separately. "Will you be running this as a paid ad?" If yes, that's a separate fee. Don't give away ad usage for free. That content reaches way more people than your organic post, and the brand should pay for that reach.
