Viral Hooks That Actually Work (200+ Examples)

Ledger & Spark • March 2026 • 9 min read

You have 2 seconds before someone scrolls past your video. Two seconds.

That's not an exaggeration. TikTok's own data shows that viewers decide within the first 1-3 seconds whether to keep watching or move on. Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts work the same way.

Your hook is the most important part of your content. Full stop. You could have the best editing, the most valuable information, the funniest punchline — but if your hook doesn't grab attention, nobody will ever see it.

This guide breaks down the psychology behind hooks that work, gives you 7 proven formulas, and shows you where to find 200+ hooks sorted by niche.

Why Hooks Work (The Psychology)

Before we get into formulas, let's talk about WHY certain hooks stop the scroll. Understanding the psychology makes you better at creating your own.

1. Curiosity Gap

When your brain encounters an incomplete piece of information, it creates tension. Your brain NEEDS to close that gap. It's almost involuntary.

"Nobody talks about this..." creates a gap. What is "this"? You need to know.

2. Pattern Interrupt

Your brain is constantly filtering information. When something breaks the expected pattern, your attention snaps to it.

Starting with a loud noise, a weird visual, or a bold claim interrupts the pattern of scrolling.

3. Self-Relevance

When something seems directly relevant to YOU, your brain prioritizes it.

"If you're a content creator with under 50K followers, read this" — if that's you, you can't scroll past.

4. Loss Aversion

Humans are more motivated by avoiding loss than by gaining something.

"Stop doing this if you want more followers" triggers the fear of making a mistake.

5. Social Proof

References to numbers, results, or what "everyone" is doing trigger the desire to conform.

"I tested this for 30 days — here's what happened" implies real results you can learn from.

7 Viral Hook Formulas

Formula 1: "Nobody talks about [topic]..."

Why it works: Pure curiosity gap. The word "nobody" implies hidden knowledge.

Examples:

"Nobody talks about how much smaller creators actually make."
"Nobody talks about the numbers that get you brand deals."
"Nobody talks about why your captions aren't working."
"Nobody talks about the real reason your posts flop."

Best for: Educational content, hot takes, myth-busting.

Formula 2: "I tested [thing] for [time period]. Here's what happened."

Why it works: Personal experiment = built-in story arc. Specificity builds credibility.

Examples:

"I tested posting 3x a day for 30 days. Here's what happened."
"I used only free tools for my content for 60 days. Here's what happened."
"I DM'd 50 brands in one week. Here's what happened."
"I changed my bio and gained 500 followers in 3 days. Here's what happened."

Best for: Results-driven content, before/after, growth stories.

Formula 3: "Stop [common mistake] if you want [desired result]."

Why it works: Loss aversion. "Am I making that mistake?"

Examples:

"Stop using hashtags like this if you want more reach."
"Stop posting at this time if you want more views."
"Stop ignoring your bio if you want brand deals."
"Stop guessing your rate if you want to get paid fairly."

Best for: Advice content, tip videos, common mistakes.

Formula 4: "The truth about [popular thing]..."

Why it works: Implies insider knowledge. Makes the viewer feel like they're about to learn something others don't know.

Examples:

"The truth about like-for-like pods."
"The truth about buying followers."
"The truth about how the algorithm works."
"The truth about brand deal rates."

Best for: Myth-busting, opinions, industry insights.

Formula 5: "[Specific number] that changed everything."

Why it works: Numbers anchor attention. Specificity = credibility.

Examples:

"One number changed my entire content strategy."
"3 tools replaced my $100/month subscription."
"This 2-second trick doubled my watch time."
"5 words that make any caption better."

Best for: Tips, hacks, transformation stories.

Formula 6: "I wish I knew this when I started [activity]."

Why it works: Regret hook. The audience doesn't want to make the same mistake.

Examples:

"I wish I knew this when I started posting on TikTok."
"I wish I knew this before my first brand deal."
"I wish I knew this about how the algorithm rates your posts."
"I wish I knew this about writing captions."

Best for: Advice content, lessons learned, beginner tips.

Formula 7: "This free tool does [impressive thing] in [short time]."

Why it works: Value + speed = irresistible. People love shortcuts that actually work.

Examples:

"This free tool writes viral hooks in 10 seconds."
"This free tool tells you what you're worth to brands instantly."
"This free tool generates 10 captions at once."
"This free tool tells you if your posts are actually doing well."

Best for: Tool demos, product content, tutorials.

How to Write Your Own Hooks

Now that you know the formulas, here's the process:

  1. Start with the formula — pick one from above
  2. Fill in YOUR topic — what's the video actually about?
  3. Make it specific — "3 tips" is better than "some tips." "30 days" is better than "a while."
  4. Read it out loud — if it sounds boring when you say it, it'll sound boring in the video
  5. Test variations — try 3-4 hooks for the same video, pick the strongest one
