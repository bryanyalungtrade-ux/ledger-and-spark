🏮 Chinatown

NYC's OG cheap eats capital. Dumplings for pocket change. Noodles that go stupid hard.

🥟Shu Jiao Fu Zhou
$3-4
Chinese / Fuzhounese
295 Grand St, Manhattan
Their $3.50 peanut noodles are genuinely unhinged. Creamy, nutty, and you'll be thinking about them for days. 10 pork & chive dumplings for $4. This is not a drill.
🔥 Must order: Peanut butter noodles + pork dumplings
Hole-in-the-wall, cash only, no frills, all flavor
🫓Mei Lai Wah
$1.50
Chinese Bakery
64 Bayard St, Manhattan
$1.50 pineapple pork buns that are legitimately life-changing. Flaky outside, savory inside. The line moves fast and it's worth every second.
🔥 Must order: Pineapple pork bun
Cash only, grab & go, old-school bakery energy
🥟Vanessa's Dumpling House
$2-5
Chinese
118A Eldridge St, Manhattan
4 fried dumplings for $2. TWO DOLLARS. They're crispy, juicy, and the sesame pancake sandwich is insane too. This is the NYC starter pack.
🔥 Must order: Fried dumplings + sesame pancake
Quick counter service, always packed, always worth it
🍜Tasty Hand-Pulled Noodles
$8-12
Chinese / Hand-pulled Noodles
1 Doyers St, Manhattan
You literally watch them pull your noodles fresh. The stir-fried knife-cut noodles are chewy perfection. This tiny spot on Doyers St has the most fire noodle game in Chinatown.
🔥 Must order: Stir fried knife-cut noodles
Tiny, chaotic, mesmerizing noodle-pulling action
🥟Jin Mei Dumplings
$5
Chinese
Chinatown, Manhattan
15 dumplings for $5. Do the math. That's $0.33 per dumpling. Absolutely ridiculous value and they taste like someone's grandma made them with love.
🔥 Must order: 15-piece dumpling order
No-nonsense, cash only, quantity AND quality
🍚Yi Ji Shi Mo
$4-8
Cantonese
Chinatown, Manhattan
The cheong fun (rice rolls) here are silky, delicate, and stuffed with pork, shrimp, or cilantro. It's one of those "if you know, you know" spots that locals gatekeep.
🔥 Must order: Shrimp rice rolls
Hidden gem, authentic Cantonese, cash only
🥖Banh Mi Saigon
$10
Vietnamese
Chinatown, Manhattan
$10 banh mi that's loaded with marinated pork, pickled carrots, daikon, cilantro, and chiles on the crispiest baguette. The bread-to-filling ratio is *chef's kiss*.
🔥 Must order: Classic pork banh mi
Sandwich perfection, quick grab, Vietnamese flavors
🫕Spicy Village
$7-15
Chinese / Henan
68 Forsyth St, Manhattan
The "big tray chicken" ($16 but feeds two = $8/person) is legendary. Hand-pulled noodles swimming in spicy, numbing sauce with tender chicken. Soup dumplings 9 for $6.75.
🔥 Must order: Big tray chicken (split it!)
Cozy, spicy, share-with-your-crew energy
💡 Pro tip: Chinatown is CASH ONLY at most spots. Hit the ATM before you go. Also — go on a weekday to skip lines.

🌎 Jackson Heights

Most diverse food mile in America. Colombian, Nepali, Mexican, Bangladeshi — all under $10.

🫓Arepa Lady
$5-10
Colombian
Jackson Heights, Queens
Started as a legendary street cart, now a restaurant. The arepa de choclo (sweet corn, folded) with chicharron (pork belly) is the move. Every arepa is great but the folded ones are easier to eat on the go.
🔥 Must order: Arepa de choclo with chicharron
Cart-turned-restaurant, Colombian icon, cash preferred
🥟Momo Crave
$8-12
Tibetan / Nepali
Jackson Heights, Queens
Tibetan momos that are straight-up addictive. Steamed, fried, or in soup — all incredible. The thukpa (noodle soup) for $8 is warming, filling, and the perfect rainy-day meal.
🔥 Must order: Fried momos + thukpa soup
Cozy Himalayan, affordable, soul-warming
🌮Birria-Landia
$4-12
Mexican / Birria
Roosevelt Ave, Jackson Heights, Queens
THE birria spot of NYC. This Tijuana-style truck gave birria the main character energy it deserved. Tacos dipped in consomme, dripping with cheese, messy in the best way. Best at night.
🔥 Must order: Birria tacos + consomme for dipping
Food truck legend, late-night, messy & perfect
🫔Mi Pequeno
$3-8
Salvadoran
Jackson Heights, Queens
Best pupusas in Queens. The Revuelta (cheese + refried beans + pork rind) is the one. Thick, stuffed corn cakes that are crispy outside and gooey inside. $3 each and incredibly filling.
🔥 Must order: Pupusa Revuelta
No-frills Salvadoran, family-run, pupusa perfection
🇧🇩Fuskahouse / Tong
$5-8
Bangladeshi
73rd St & Roosevelt, Jackson Heights
Bangladeshi food truck outside Duane Reade. Their fuchka — puffed puri filled with peas and potatoes, topped with raw onions and shaved egg yolks — is unlike anything else in NYC. $5 and mind-blowing.
🔥 Must order: Fuchka
Food truck, Bangladeshi street food, totally unique
🥐La Gran Uruguaya
$3-7
Uruguayan Bakery
Jackson Heights, Queens
Uruguayan flags, soccer on TV, and fresh-baked pastries everywhere. The Milhoja (puff pastry layered with dulce de leche) is insane. Not a restaurant — it's a bakery experience. Get the empanadas too.
🔥 Must order: Milhoja + beef empanada
Soccer on TV, pastry heaven, Uruguayan pride
💡 Pro tip: Walk Roosevelt Ave for the full food crawl. Bring cash (many spots are cash-only). Start at Junction Blvd 7 train and eat your way east.

🏯 Flushing

NYC's real Chinatown. $4 dumplings, $10 feasts, and food courts that'll blow your mind.

🥟White Bear
$5-8
Chinese
135-02 Roosevelt Ave, Flushing
A tiny takeout window with 34 menu options and arguably the best dumplings in all of NYC. Order #6 — wontons with hot sauce. 12 per order, soft, chewy, and drenched in chili oil. This is the one.
🔥 Must order: #6 — Wontons with hot sauce
Takeout window, cult following, cash only
🥟Zhu Ji Dumpling Stall
$4
Chinese
40-52 Main St, Flushing
8 fried dumplings for $4. Scallion pancake $1.50. Pork bun $1.50. Soybean milk $1.50. You can literally get ALL of that for $10 total. This math is unmatched.
🔥 Must order: Fried dumplings + scallion pancake
Stall vibes, insane value, the $10 challenge spot
🏬New World Mall Food Court
$5-12
Chinese / Various Asian
136-20 Roosevelt Ave, Flushing
Massive underground food court with dozens of vendors. Dumplings, scallion pancakes, noodles, skewers, bubble tea — it's like a theme park but for your stomach. Go before noon to beat crowds.
🔥 Must order: A little of everything (food court crawl)
Food court maze, overwhelming (in a good way), arrive hungry
🍜Lanzhou Handmade Noodle
$8-12
Chinese / Lanzhou
Flushing Food Court, Queens
Hand-pulled noodles made right in front of you. The beef noodle soup is the star — clear broth, chewy noodles, tender beef. Simple, perfect, and under $12. This is noodle soup therapy.
🔥 Must order: Beef hand-pulled noodle soup
Noodle-pulling show, food court gem, warming
🥟Shanghai You Garden
$8-14
Shanghainese
Flushing, Queens
Order the Peking Duck Bun and you get 6 pork soup dumplings FREE. Yes, free. The soup dumplings are bursting with hot broth. Cash only and always worth the trip to Flushing.
🔥 Must order: Peking Duck Bun (free soup dumplings!)
Cash only, soup dumpling masters, free bonus dumplings
💡 Pro tip: Take the 7 train to Flushing-Main St. Bring CASH — most spots are cash only. The food court in New World Mall basement is the move.
